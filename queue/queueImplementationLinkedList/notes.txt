
in stack 
You are adding a node to the front of the list.

You need the new node to point to the current head (the rest of the stack).

The Code: node.next = this.head; (You are setting the new node's pointer).
In the Stack, the new node is the "active" one doing the work of connecting. That is why you write node.next = ...


in queue
You are adding a node to the back of the list.

You are not changing the new node's pointer (it already points to null from the constructor).

Instead, you need to change the previous last node (the rear) so that its .next points to your new node.

The Code: this.rear.next = node;

In the Queue, the rear node is the "active" one doing the work of connecting to the new person. That is why you write 
this.rear.next = ...
