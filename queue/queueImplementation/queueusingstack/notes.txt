enqueue is simple :- just push the x this.stack.push(x);
dequeue is complex :- Use recursion to reach the bottom element then push one by one in stack so bottom elem come at top


Visualization: dequeue() when Stack is [10, 20, 30]

The "Unwinding" Phase (Popping down to the bottom)

dequeue() Call 1:
this.stack = [10, 20, 30]
pop(30) → temp = 30
Calling this.dequeue() recursively...

dequeue() Call 2:
this.stack = [10, 20]
pop(20) → temp = 20
Calling this.dequeue() recursively...

dequeue() Call 3 (The Base Case):
this.stack = [10]
It sees length === 1
pop(10) → res = 10
This function returns 10

The "Rebuilding" Phase (Function returns)

Returning to dequeue() Call 2:
Received res = 10
Now, it must restore the stack:
this.stack.push(temp)  (temp was 20)
this.stack = [20]
This function returns 10

Returning to dequeue() Call 1:
Received res = 10
Now, it must restore the stack:
this.stack.push(temp)  (temp was 30)
this.stack = [20, 30]

The main function returns 10

Final Result:

Queue Behavior:
The oldest item (10) was successfully removed

Stack State:
The remaining items (20, 30) are back in their correct, original order
