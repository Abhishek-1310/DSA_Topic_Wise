Here is the breakdown of how we arrive at the complexity of $O(\log n)$.
 Start: $n$ elements = 8
Step 1: $n/2$ elements remaining = 4
Step 2: $n/4$ elements remaining (which is $n/2^2$) = 2
Step 3: $n/8$ elements remaining (which is $n/2^3$)  = 1 
Step k:  n/2^k elements remaining 
Start: n / 2^k = 1
Multiply both sides by 2^k: n = 2^k
Take the logarithm (base 2) of both sides: log2(n) = log2(2^k)
Using log rules: k = log2(n)
 In most programming languages, integers have a maximum limit.
The formula mid = (st + ed) / 2 first calculates (st + ed).
If st and ed are both very large, their sum exceeds the maximum limit, causing an overflow.
Result: The number wraps around to a negative value, leading to an "Index Out of Bounds" error or a crash.
Solution is  let mid = Math.floor(st + (ed - st) / 2);
