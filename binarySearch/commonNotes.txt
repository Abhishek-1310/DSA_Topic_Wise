mid is eliminated immediately — you do r = mid - 1 or l = mid + 1

Use l < r when:
You're searching for a condition/position (peak, minimum, boundary)
mid is not immediately eliminated — you keep it with r = mid

Ask ONE question:
Can mid still be the answer?

CASE 1
Mid CANNOT be answer
Then discard it.
Use:
while(l <= r)
r = mid - 1
l = mid + 1

Why? Because: you completely eliminate mid
Typical problems: Normal Binary Search /  Lower Bound / Upper Bound /Search Target

CASE 2  // do case 2 when "Did my comparison prove mid cannot be answer?"
Mid MAY still be answer
Keep it
Use
while(l < r)
r = mid
or
l = mid

Why?
Because:
you are NOT sure mid is wrong
Typical problems:
Find Minimum /Find Peak /First Bad Version / Search Space Reduction

Only discard mid when you are 100% sure
mid cannot be the answer.
