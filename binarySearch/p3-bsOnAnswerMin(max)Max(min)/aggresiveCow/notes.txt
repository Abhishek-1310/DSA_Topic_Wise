Aggressive Cows
You are given an array with unique elements of stalls[], which denote the positions of stalls. You are also 
given an integer k which denotes the number of aggressive cows. The task is to assign stalls to k cows such that
 the minimum distance between any two of them is the maximum possible.

Examples:

Input: stalls[] = [1, 2, 4, 8, 9], k = 3
                   0, 1, 2, 3, 4
Output: 3
Explanation: The first cow can be placed at stalls[0], 
the second cow can be placed at stalls[2] and 
the third cow can be placed at stalls[3]. 
The minimum distance between cows in this case is 3, which is the largest among all possible ways.

how to find min;
Let's trace it with your example:stalls = [1, 2, 4, 8, 9], k = 3, 
and let's test mid = 3 
.Cow 1: Place at stalls[0] (Position 1). lastPosition = 1.
Check stall at 2: Is 2 - 1 >= 3 No. (Skip it).
Check stall at 4: Is 4 - 1 >= 3$? Yes !Cow 2: Place at position 4. lastPosition = 4.
Check stall at 8: Is 8 - 4 >= 3$? Yes !Cow 3: Place at position 8. lastPosition = 8.Result: We placed 3 cows. Return true.
When the function returns true for a mid of 3, it guarantees that the minimum distance between any two cows is at least 3.
Some cows might be further apart (like the cows at 4 and 8, which are 4 apart).
But none are closer than 3.
Therefore, the "minimum distance" for that specific arrangement is at least 3.

to find max of all possibility
The "Max of Min" Strategy
Initialize ans: Create a variable (let’s call it result) to store the largest distance that actually worked.

The Range:
low = 1 (The smallest possible gap). not first stall maybe 1st stall at 10 but min dist between 2 stall 1;
high = stalls[last] - stalls[0] (The largest possible gap). between two stall after sort array

The Loop:
Calculate mid.
Run your canPlaceCows(mid) function.

If it returns true: This means a minimum distance of mid is possible! But is there a bigger one?
Update result = mid.
Move low = mid + 1 to try and find a larger "minimum."
If it returns false: This gap is too wide for the number of cows you have.
Move high = mid - 1 to shrink the gap.
