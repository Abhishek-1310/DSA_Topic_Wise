N-Queens
The n-queens puzzle is the problem of placing n queens on an n x n chessboard such that no two queens attack each other.
Given an integer n, return all distinct solutions to the n-queens puzzle. You may return the answer in any order.
Each solution contains a distinct board configuration of the n-queens' placement, where 'Q' and '.' both indicate a queen and an empty space, respectively.

The Outer Layer (Array.from) decides how many "shelves" (rows) you are building.
The Inner Layer (Array(n).fill('.')) builds a brand new "tray" (column set) and fills it with dots before sliding it onto the shelf.

Top-Left to Bottom-Right
On a 4 × 4 board, the diagonal "names" (row - col values) are:

-3: (0,3)
-2: (0,2), (1,3)
-1: (0,1), (1,2), (2,3)
 0: (0,0), (1,1), (2,2), (3,3)  ← Main diagonal
 1: (1,0), (2,1), (3,2)
 2: (2,0), (3,1)
 3: (3,0)

Top-Right to Bottom-Left
On a 4 × 4 board, the diagonal "names" (row + col values) are:
0: (0,0)
1: (0,1), (1,0)
2: (0,2), (1,1), (2,0)
3: (0,3), (1,2), (2,1), (3,0)
4: (1,3), (2,2), (3,1)
5: (2,3), (3,2)
6: (3,3)
